\newcommand{\pubabstract}{%
%
This paper presents a novel architectural approach to actor systems by
decoupling mailboxes from processing actors. We propose promoting mailboxes to
independent actor entities specialising in message management while processing
actors focusing solely on their core functionality. This separation enables
flexible message scheduling, modular mailbox implementations, and improved
scalability. We formally specify the system's operational semantics, which
captures this separation of concerns and enables rigorous reasoning about the
system's behavior. The design addresses challenges in managing high-volume
message flows while maintaining modularity. Although this approach may introduce
additional complexity and potential latency, we argue that the gains in
flexibility and maintainability of this mailbox-as-actor-based systems outweigh
these trade-offs.
}
