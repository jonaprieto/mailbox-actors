\newcommand{\pubabstract}{%
%
This paper presents a novel architectural approach to
actor systems by decoupling mailboxes from processing actors. We propose
modeling mailboxes as independent actor entities that specialize in message
management, while processing actors focus solely on their core functionality.
This separation enables flexible message scheduling, modular mailbox
implementations, and improved scalability. We provide a formal specification of
the message-passing mechanism and operational semantics for this decoupled
architecture. The design addresses challenges in message flow management under
high volumes while maintaining clear separation of concerns. Although the
approach may introduce additional system complexity and latency, we believe
the benefits in terms of flexibility, scalability, and maintainability of
actor-based systems are worth of the possible trade-offs.
}
